## Ingredients

| Ingredient                        | Quantity |
|-----------------------------------|----------|
| Unsalted butter (room temperature)| 1 cup    |
| Dark brown sugar                  | 1½ cups  |
| Large eggs                        | 2        |
| Extra egg yolk                    | 1        |
| Pure vanilla extract              | 2 tsp    |
| All-purpose flour                 | 2¼ cups  |
| Baking soda                       | 1 tsp    |
| Fine sea salt                     | 1 tsp    |
| Flaky sea salt (for topping)      | ½ tsp    |
| Dark chocolate chips (60% cacao)  | 2 cups   |

> **Intervention:** 100% dark brown sugar (vs. standard 1 cup brown + ½ cup white).
> **Control batch:** standard recipe with mixed sugars.

## Procedure

1. Creamed butter and dark brown sugar together until light and fluffy (~3 min).
2. Added 2 whole eggs, 1 extra yolk, and vanilla extract. Whisked vigorously for 90 seconds.
3. Folded in flour, baking soda, and fine sea salt until just combined.
4. Folded in dark chocolate chips.
5. Rested dough at room temperature for 30 minutes.
6. Preheated oven to 375°F / 190°C.
7. Portioned into 2-tablespoon rounds spaced 5 cm apart. Topped each with a pinch of flaky salt.
8. Baked 11 minutes. Edges golden; centers appeared underdone (correct).
9. Cooled on baking sheet 5 minutes, then wire rack 20 minutes before evaluation.
10. Baked control batch in parallel.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental:** Final spread ~4.2 cm. Deeper amber-brown color than control. Aroma noticeably more molasses-forward during baking. Flavor: pronounced caramel and toffee with a slight bitterness at the finish. Texture: chewier and more dense than control; edges slightly less crisp.
- **Control:** Final spread ~4.5 cm. Standard color and flavor profile. Crispier edge.
- Experimental cookies softened more overnight than control — high moisture content of dark brown sugar appears to accelerate this.
- 4 out of 5 evaluators preferred experimental for flavor intensity; 1 preferred control for texture.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **partially confirmed**. Dark brown sugar did deepen caramel flavor and increase chewiness as expected. However, the increased moisture content caused the cookies to soften faster overnight, which is an undesirable trade-off for texture longevity.

Recommended next step: test a 70/30 split (dark brown / white) to retain flavor improvement while recovering some crispness and shelf stability.
